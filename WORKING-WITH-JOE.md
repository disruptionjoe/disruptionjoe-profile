# Working With Joe

This is a practical guide to how I work /my style, strengths, preferences, and failure modes. Use this to understand whether we are a good fit and how to get the most out of working together.

For service offerings and pricing, see [SERVICES.md](SERVICES.md). For professional background, see [RESUME.md](RESUME.md).

---

## How I Think

I am strong in ambiguity. I discuss first, then decide. I think quickly and intuitively, refine after the first directional judgment, and bias toward action.

I want options and tradeoffs on the table, but I make sure we actually decide and move. I am data-informed, not data-paralyzed. Reversible decisions get made quickly. Irreversible ones get more care.

I hold strong opinions loosely. I may speak clearly and confidently, but that does not mean I think my first view is sacred.

---

## Where I Create the Most Value

I am at my best when I am brought in early, given a meaningful challenge rather than a narrow task list, and allowed to help turn ambiguity into a clear problem, strong decisions, and forward momentum.

The most natural entry point is: help us move from messy discussion to clear next steps.

Other good uses: frame the situation, define the real problem, pressure-test our thinking, facilitate a group, decide between options, bridge from problem framing into execution, own a clearly defined workstream.

Facilitation is one of my strongest tools. I have spoken at ETHDenver, led 100+ meetups, and run sessions with audiences ranging from retirees to startup founders. I am comfortable explaining complex systems to mixed audiences and keeping a room productive.

---

## Getting Oriented

When I join a project, I want to understand five things in this order:

1. Who has decision rights
2. What challenge is creating the need for this work
3. What the real problem is
4. What success looks like
5. Who the stakeholders are

A lot of teams move too fast from challenge to activity without clearly defining the problem. I do my best work when we take enough time to confirm we are aimed at the right thing.

---

## Communication

I work best with communication that is direct, concise, structured, asynchronous by default, and clear about what action is being requested.

I appreciate when communication labels whether something is a request, a suggestion, context, a decision already made, or something still open for input.

My preferred pattern: written first, live discussion second if needed. I get a lot out of conversation when the goal is sense-making or alignment.

---

## Working Rhythm

Deep work in the morning. Calls and interactive work in the afternoon. Routine and schedule over chaos. Strong sprint capacity, but not indefinitely. Some recovery after socially intense events.

I prefer sharing an 80% version early to confirm directional alignment, then refining from feedback. That usually produces better outcomes than disappearing and trying to perfect something in isolation.

---

## ADHD Context

I have ADHD and use structure to compensate. Systems, routines, and clear decision rights help me do my best work. I built CapacityOS /a cadence-driven agent operating system with explicit handoff and dashboard-first steering /partly because I needed an operating system that works with how my brain works.

This is not a limitation. It is the reason I am unusually good at building structure for ambiguous environments. I know what it takes to turn chaos into clarity because I have to do it for myself every day.

---

## What Drains Me

Tedious admin, documentation overhead, meticulous polish passes, operational cleanup as a primary function, and relationship management or lead generation as the main job.

I do not mind follow-through and accountability when I am empowered to shape outcomes and hold people accountable. What drains me is being personally buried in low-leverage follow-through that pulls me away from where I create the most value.

---

## Feedback

I want feedback that is direct, clear, specific, early, and focused on outcomes. Blunt is fine if it is useful. Small course-corrections early beat silent frustration.

---

## Common Failure Modes

Ways that create avoidable friction:

- Bringing me in too late, or only for execution instead of framing
- Giving me responsibility without enough authority
- Asking me to clean up messes I could have helped prevent earlier
- Overriding my ownership through side directives or back-channel decisions
- Allowing endless discussion without decisions
- Leaving decision rights or success criteria vague

---

## How to Tell If It Is Working

**Going well:** Work is shipping, decisions are getting made, momentum is building, the work feels meaningful, I am engaged and contributive.

**Not going well:** I start wanting to re-litigate decisions, the work starts to feel pointless, I stop volunteering input, my sense of ownership gets undermined. The more likely pattern is that I become less energized and less proactive because the collaboration no longer feels meaningful.

---

## Common Misreads

- Intensity can be mistaken for frustration
- Fast thinking can be mistaken for shallow thinking
- Confidence can be mistaken for certainty
- Broad context-sharing can be mistaken for noise

My intent is usually to improve clarity, momentum, and outcomes. If my style is not landing well, I want to know.
