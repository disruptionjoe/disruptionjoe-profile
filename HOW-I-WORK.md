# How I Work

This document is for anyone considering working with me — as a collaborator, client, employer, or teammate. It covers how I think, how I communicate, what I'm strong at, and what to expect.

---

## Working style

I operate best in environments with high ambiguity and real stakes. I'm the person you bring in when the situation is unclear, the team is overwhelmed, or there are too many options and not enough action. I turn that into structure, clarity, and momentum.

I'm a generalist by design, not by accident. I've chosen to build breadth across strategy, operations, product, and growth because the most valuable work usually happens at the intersections. I connect dots that specialists miss because they're looking at different maps.

I default to action. If I can start something useful right now, I'd rather do that and iterate than spend a week planning the perfect version. I'm comfortable being wrong early if it means being useful sooner.

## Communication

Direct, clear, low-ego. I say what I think and I ask real questions. I don't perform certainty when I don't have it, and I don't soften feedback to the point where it stops being useful.

I write well and think clearly in writing. A lot of my best work happens in docs, not meetings. I'm a strong facilitator in meetings when they're needed — I help groups move from circular discussion to concrete decisions.

I prefer async communication for most things. Meetings should be for alignment, decisions, and creative work — not status updates.

## Strengths

**Systems thinking.** I see how the parts connect and where the leverage points are. When a team is stuck, it's usually a structural problem disguised as a people problem or a motivation problem. I find the structural fix.

**Translating strategy to execution.** I can hold a high-level vision and break it down into a concrete plan that a team can actually run. I don't just say "we should do X" — I figure out who does what, in what order, and what happens when it doesn't work.

**Working in ambiguity.** I don't need perfect information to start. I can hold multiple hypotheses, make bets, test them, and adjust. This is especially useful in early-stage, fast-moving, or under-resourced environments.

**Making AI practically useful.** I understand AI agents and workflows at the operational level — not as hype but as tools. I can help teams figure out where AI actually reduces work, where it creates noise, and how to build systems that make the collaboration productive.

**Facilitation and alignment.** I help groups that are stuck, talking past each other, or overwhelmed by options get to clarity. I do this by naming the real question, separating what's decided from what's still open, and creating structure that makes progress visible.

## ADHD context

I have ADHD. I'm open about it because it shapes how I work and because pretending otherwise doesn't help anyone.

What this means in practice: I use systems and structure heavily to stay on track. I've built my own AI-assisted operating system specifically to compensate for executive function challenges. I'm stronger with clear priorities than with open-ended ambiguity that has no stakes. I sometimes need to manage energy and attention deliberately rather than powering through.

What it does not mean: I am not unreliable, inconsistent, or incapable of sustained effort. I have built companies, led complex operations, and delivered at high levels. The systems I use to manage ADHD are the same systems I bring to teams I work with — because they work for everyone, not just neurodivergent people.

I mention this because the best working relationships start with honesty about how someone actually operates, not a performance of how they wish they did.

## What I need from a working environment

- Clear priorities (or the mandate to set them)
- Autonomy to figure out the how
- Feedback that is direct, not wrapped in layers of politeness
- A team that values outcomes over process theater
- Room to build systems, not just execute tasks

## What you can expect from me

- Clarity about what I think, even when it's uncomfortable
- Follow-through on commitments, supported by real systems
- A bias toward action and iteration
- Strong written communication
- The ability to hold both the big picture and the operational details
