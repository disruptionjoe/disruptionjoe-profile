I design and test AI-native systems for better human judgment, coordination, legitimacy, and execution.

My work has three connected lanes:

- **Practice** - the methods I use with real teams and leaders.

## Practice

Practice is the client-facing layer of the work: facilitated rooms, reusable methods, and enablement architecture that help people build real AI capability.

Two public surfaces matter most:

**[AI Activation Playbook](https://disruptionjoe.com/playbook/)**

The Playbook is the method behind facilitated AI sessions: arcs, plays, moves, room dynamics, and exercises that help teams practice with AI on work that matters.

**[AI Enablement Architecture](https://disruptionjoe.com/enablement/)**

Enablement Architecture asks what happens after people gain confidence: how an organization makes AI-supported work visible, repeatable, measurable, governed, and valuable enough to compound.

The practical question is not "Can people use AI?" The better question is:

> Can people, teams, and organizations use AI in ways that improve judgment, coordination, throughput, and business value without losing accountability?

For services and commercial entry points, start with [disruptionjoe.com/services](https://disruptionjoe.com/services/).

## Passion

Passion is the lab. It is where I build systems for myself and push questions to the edge.

Some active public threads:

**[Caret^](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/caret)**

An open-source semantic notation for agent workflows in Markdown. It restores expressivity to prompts with scalar directives and layered scope. Token savings are useful; clearer intent is the point.

**[Geometric Unity formalization](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/gu-formalization)**

Can a bold contested claim be evaluated with enough structure, receipts, and adversarial pressure that the work becomes truth-seeking instead of vibes?

**[Temporal Issuance](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/temporal-issuance)**

Can a strange originating idea become disciplined enough to produce useful formal and conceptual artifacts?

**[Time as Finality](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/time-as-finality)**

Can cross-disciplinary research pull distinct frames together without dissolving into metaphor?

**[Architecture of Legitimacy](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/architecture-of-legitimacy)**

Can legitimacy, contribution, governance, incentives, and capture resistance be treated as design problems?

Some work is public. Some is private or not ready for direct linking. When a system is not ready to be exposed directly, I prefer to route through a public explanation rather than pretend the private artifact is public proof.

For a more designed tour of the lab, see [AI Accelerated Thinking](https://disruptionjoe.com/thinking/).

## Purpose

The purpose underneath the work is simple to say and hard to practice:

> Build systems that increase human agency rather than replace it.

I care about AI because it changes how people think, coordinate, decide, govern, learn, and act together. The opportunity is not only more output. It is better judgment, better cooperation, better evidence, better legitimacy, and better institutions.

That only works if the systems make people more capable, not more dependent.

The public version of this story lives at [About Joe](https://disruptionjoe.com/about/). The longer version is in [WHO-IS-JOE.md](WHO-IS-JOE.md).
